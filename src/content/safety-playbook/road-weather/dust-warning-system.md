The Dual Use Safety Technology (DUST) Warning System is designed to address weather-related challenges along Interstate 10 in Arizona, particularly in areas prone to dust storms and winter icing. Developed and deployed by the Arizona Department of Transportation, the system provides early detection and warning capabilities that help drivers and transportation managers respond more effectively to hazardous road conditions.

One of the system's primary functions is detection of visibility hazards caused by blowing dust. Wind-driven dust can rapidly reduce visibility to near zero, creating extremely dangerous driving conditions and increasing the risk of severe multi-vehicle crashes. The DUST Warning System uses weather and visibility sensors to identify these conditions in real time and alert drivers before they enter affected areas.

The system integrates advanced remote sensing equipment that can automatically influence traffic operations during hazardous conditions. When low visibility is detected, the system can help manage traffic by controlling variable speed limits, reducing sudden braking, minimizing delays, and lowering crash likelihood.

Automated visibility warnings are delivered through roadside message signs and traveler information tools. The system also communicates with traffic management centers, allowing officials to monitor conditions, coordinate responses, and disseminate accurate information through integrated traveler information channels.
