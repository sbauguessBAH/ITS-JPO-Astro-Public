The eSTORM system is designed to enhance traffic management and operational efficiency before, during, and after severe storms and hurricane events. It provides transportation agencies and emergency managers with tools to quickly assess damage, prioritize response actions, and restore normal traffic operations as safely and efficiently as possible.

A primary function of eSTORM is rapid restoration of traffic signals damaged by extreme weather. Through its mobile application, field personnel can immediately record signal status, damage levels, and repair needs. This real-time data entry allows maintenance crews and traffic engineers to identify critical locations and dispatch resources where they are most needed.

The system also supports removal of lane-blocking debris that can disrupt traffic flow and hinder emergency response. By documenting debris locations and roadway conditions, agencies can coordinate cleanup efforts and reopen lanes more quickly.

For longer-term closures, eSTORM supports detour management by helping agencies plan, implement, and monitor alternate routes, maintaining continuity of travel while supporting emergency operations and recovery.

The combination of a mobile application, statewide traffic signal database, and real-time dashboards provides a comprehensive view of system conditions, enabling informed decisions based on current network data.
