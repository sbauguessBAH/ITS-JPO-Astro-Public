Road Weather Information Systems (RWIS) play a vital role in transportation operations by providing accurate, real-time, and predictive information about weather and road conditions. These systems support transportation managers, road operators, and maintenance crews by enabling data-driven decisions that enhance safety, reduce operational costs, and maintain efficient traffic flow, particularly during winter weather events.

The data provided by RWIS is used by transportation agencies at both national and local levels to support proactive planning and response. During winter weather, RWIS enables early mobilization of maintenance forces, more efficient use of materials and equipment, and improved coordination of operations.

A core component of RWIS is the Environmental Sensor Station (ESS), which serves as physical infrastructure for collecting critical weather and road condition data. The ESS is equipped with sensors that monitor and record atmospheric, pavement, and water-level conditions, including air temperature, humidity, wind speed and direction, precipitation type and intensity, visibility, lightning activity, and storm-cell movement.

In addition to atmospheric monitoring, RWIS collects detailed pavement data that is critical for winter maintenance decision-making. Pavement sensors measure surface temperature, freezing point, surface condition (wet, icy, flooded), chemical concentration from treatment applications, and subsurface conditions. This information helps agencies determine when roads are likely to become hazardous and guides anti-icing and deicing treatment timing.

RWIS can also monitor water-level data for streams, rivers, lakes, and tidal areas near roadways. This helps identify flood risks, protect transportation infrastructure, and support safer travel in high-water-prone areas.
