Machine Learning Assessment for Traffic Signals provides an intelligent framework for evaluating and improving intersection operations using both real-time and historical data.

The approach combines inputs from detectors, cameras, connected-vehicle feeds, and controller logs to quantify how signals perform across timing plans, demand conditions, and multimodal interactions. Machine-learning models identify inefficiencies such as recurring queues, delay imbalances, and phase allocation issues, then generate recommendations for operational and timing improvements.

Because models can detect trends and forecast likely congestion or disruption, agencies can move from reactive tuning to proactive optimization. This can improve travel time reliability, reduce stop-and-go conditions, and support safer accommodation of pedestrians, cyclists, transit, and general traffic.

Cloud-based analytics environments also allow corridor- and network-level performance monitoring, enabling agencies to track outcomes related to safety, emissions, delay, and throughput over time.

Implementation and communication involve high-resolution field data capture and reliable transfer of detector and controller information to analytics platforms. Within those platforms, ML workflows evaluate patterns, reveal operational gaps, and support targeted timing or control strategy updates.
