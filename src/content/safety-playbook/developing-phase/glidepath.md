Glidepath is a transportation system enhancement that uses advanced communication and sensor technologies to improve traffic flow and fuel efficiency near signalized intersections.

By combining GPS tracking, signal phase and timing (SPaT) messages, and vehicle-to-infrastructure (V2I) communication, Glidepath provides drivers with guidance on the optimal speed needed to synchronize with traffic signal changes. This approach can reduce stop-and-go driving by helping vehicles pass through green phases with fewer abrupt speed changes.

The system relies on connected vehicle platforms that continuously evaluate speed, location, and upcoming signal timing. For example, a vehicle approaching an intersection may receive an advisory to decelerate slightly so arrival better aligns with a green phase. This can reduce hard braking events, lower red-light running risk, improve intersection safety, and support smoother traffic operations.

Glidepath also supports sustainability goals by reducing fuel consumption and emissions associated with repeated acceleration and deceleration. Integration with adaptive signal control can further improve corridor-level performance and create more predictable travel conditions for all users.

Implementation and communication for Glidepath typically combine roadside units, signal controllers, and V2I data exchange so approaching vehicles receive speed guidance tied to real-time signal timing. Wireless V2X channels, including DSRC, C-V2X, or cellular networks, carry SPaT and MAP information between infrastructure and equipped vehicles to reduce unnecessary stops.
