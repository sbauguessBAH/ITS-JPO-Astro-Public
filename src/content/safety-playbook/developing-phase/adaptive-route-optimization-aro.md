Adaptive Route Optimization (ARO) is a dynamic routing approach that continuously updates route recommendations based on real-time transportation system conditions.

Unlike static routing, ARO evaluates congestion, incidents, work zones, weather impacts, and demand shifts as they occur. AI-enabled routing and predictive analytics use these inputs to recommend paths that prioritize travel-time reliability, safety, and operational efficiency across personal, freight, and transit trips.

When disruptions occur, such as crashes or lane blockages, ARO can rapidly identify alternatives and distribute updated guidance through in-vehicle systems, mobile applications, and roadside messaging. Predictive components can also anticipate surges tied to events or recurring patterns, helping reduce bottlenecks before they fully develop.

By reducing unnecessary delay and stop-and-go behavior, ARO can lower fuel use and emissions while supporting broader corridor and network management objectives.

Implementation and communication use field equipment to collect conditions on congestion, incidents, weather, and demand. Fiber, cellular, and networked data platforms carry traffic states, model outputs, route updates, and alerts across operational systems and traveler-facing channels.
