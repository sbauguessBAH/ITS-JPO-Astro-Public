The Optimization Management Model for Incident Response combines detection technologies, communications, and decision analytics to improve how agencies identify and manage roadway incidents.

This model ingests data from field sensors, cameras, connected-vehicle feeds, and crowd-sourced reports to detect incidents quickly and assess likely impact. Based on real-time conditions, operations teams can prioritize response actions and assign resources such as responders, towing, and maintenance to minimize delay and reduce secondary crash risk.

Automated dispatch support, cloud-based operations dashboards, and predictive analytics help agencies coordinate across organizations and focus on high-impact events. Emergency vehicle preemption and dynamic traveler messaging can further improve response time and reduce network disruption during active incidents.

The framework also supports preventive strategies by using historical and machine-learning models to identify high-risk places and time windows, enabling pre-staging and readiness planning.

Implementation and communication depend on outdoor sensing and connected-vehicle data for incident recognition and traffic impact tracking, combined with TMC-based optimization tools that generate recommended response plans. Fiber and cellular communication channels support data exchange among agencies, operations centers, and emergency response partners.
