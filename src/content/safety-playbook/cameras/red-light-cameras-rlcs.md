Red Light Cameras are advanced traffic safety and law enforcement tools designed to detect and document vehicles that run red lights. These systems are typically installed at busy intersections with high crash rates and are intended to enhance road safety by discouraging dangerous behavior at traffic signals.

They operate using a combination of technologies, including inductive loop sensors embedded in the pavement, radar or lidar sensors, and pole-mounted detection devices.

When a vehicle crosses the stop line after the signal turns red, the system captures multiple images or video footage, usually including one image of the vehicle entering the intersection and another image of the vehicle within the intersection. Each capture includes the vehicle's license plate, location, and date-time information.

Law enforcement personnel review the recorded evidence before a citation is mailed to the registered owner. Research shows that Red Light Cameras are effective in reducing right-angle crashes and total injury crashes at treated intersections. Modern RLC systems can also integrate with traffic management platforms so agencies can monitor violations, analyze crash trends, and improve enforcement efficiency.
