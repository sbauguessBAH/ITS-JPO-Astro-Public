Red Light Cameras are advanced traffic safety and law enforcement tools designed to detect and document vehicles that run red lights. These systems are typically installed at busy intersections with high crash rates and are intended to enhance road safety by discouraging dangerous behavior at traffic signals.

They operate using a combination of technologies, including inductive loop sensors embedded in the pavement, radar or lidar sensors, and pole-mounted detection devices.

When a vehicle crosses the stop line after the signal turns red, the system captures multiple images or video footage, usually including one image of the vehicle entering the intersection and another image of the vehicle within the intersection. Each capture includes the vehicle's license plate, location, and date-time information.

Law enforcement personnel review the recorded evidence before a citation is mailed to the registered vehicle owner, who may challenge the citation if they were not the driver at the time of the violation. Research shows that Red Light Cameras are effective in reducing right-angle crashes, right-angle injury crashes, and total injury crashes at treated intersections. Although some studies note a possible increase in rear-end collisions due to sudden braking, the overall safety impact of RLC deployments is positive. Modern RLC systems can integrate with traffic management platforms, enabling agencies to monitor violations, analyze crash trends, and improve enforcement efficiency. Advances in detection, imaging, and communication technologies continue to strengthen the role of RLCs in promoting compliance with traffic signals and enhancing intersection safety.
