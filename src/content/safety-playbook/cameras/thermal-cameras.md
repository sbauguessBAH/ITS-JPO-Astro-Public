Thermal cameras are powerful tools in transportation systems, providing enhanced monitoring and safety capabilities by detecting heat signatures from vehicles, infrastructure, and pedestrians.

In public transportation, these cameras help identify incidents such as overheating components or stopped vehicles, allowing operators to respond quickly and prevent crashes.

Railway systems benefit from thermal imaging by detecting people or obstacles on tracks, reducing the risk of collisions and improving overall safety. On roadways, thermal cameras enhance monitoring for roadside maintenance and can identify heat patterns that may indicate potential mechanical or infrastructure issues.

They are particularly valuable for pedestrian protection, allowing drivers and agencies to better detect pedestrians and other objects in low-light or nighttime conditions.

In addition to safety applications, thermal cameras support infrastructure monitoring by assessing road conditions and detecting hazards, contributing to more efficient maintenance and safer travel.
