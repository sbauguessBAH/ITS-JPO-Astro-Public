Queue Warning Systems are advanced traffic management tools that enhance road safety and optimize traffic flow by providing real-time alerts about slowdowns, stopped vehicles, or potential congestion ahead. These systems use technologies such as radar sensors, loop detectors, cameras, and wireless communication networks to monitor traffic conditions continuously.

When a queue or sudden reduction in speed is detected, the system activates dynamic warning signs, flashing lights, or variable message displays to alert drivers, giving them sufficient time to react and avoid collisions.

These systems are especially valuable in work zones, where lane changes and construction activities create higher crash risks. On highways and freeways, queue warning systems have been shown to reduce crash rates and near-crashes while improving travel times by enabling smoother flow.

Roads with poor visibility, such as sharp curves, steep grades, or areas affected by fog or darkness, also benefit from these systems because drivers receive early warnings that compensate for limited sight distance.

Beyond safety, queue warning systems support incident management, dynamic lane assignment, and other active traffic management strategies. By collecting traffic data, they help agencies analyze congestion patterns, implement timely interventions, and communicate critical information to drivers.
