The Multi-Agency Radio Communication System (MARCS) is a critical public safety communications platform used by first responders and public safety agencies throughout Ohio. Designed to support both daily operations and large-scale emergency events, MARCS provides a unified and reliable communication system that allows agencies to work together seamlessly when it matters most.

The system allows local, county, state, and federal agencies to communicate directly with one another, regardless of jurisdiction or discipline. This capability is essential during emergencies that require coordinated response efforts, such as natural disasters, major incidents, or large public events. MARCS operates on a statewide platform that provides coverage across all 88 counties in Ohio, as well as within approximately a 10 mile radius beyond the state's borders. This extensive coverage ensures continuous communication for responders as they move across jurisdictions, supporting uninterrupted operations during critical situations.

The system uses advanced radio technology to deliver secure and dependable wireless communication for public safety users. This technology enhances reliability, reduces interference, and helps protect sensitive communications, ensuring that responders can depend on the system during both routine and high stress situations.

In addition to voice communication, MARCS supports resource coordination through features such as GPS based automatic vehicle location and resource recommendation tools. These capabilities help dispatchers and emergency managers track units in real time, deploy resources more efficiently, and improve situational awareness during incidents.
