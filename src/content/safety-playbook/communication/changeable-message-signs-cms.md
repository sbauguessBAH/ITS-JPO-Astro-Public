Changeable Message Signs (CMS) are electronic traffic control devices used by transportation agencies to deliver real-time traveler information directly to motorists. CMS are a critical tool for enhancing roadway safety and improving traffic flow by providing timely, clear, and actionable messages about current and upcoming roadway conditions, particularly during nonrecurring events such as crashes, severe weather, construction, or special events.

Careful selection and construction of CMS messages help drivers make informed travel decisions, adjust driving behavior, reduce sudden braking, and choose alternate routes when necessary. When used effectively, CMS can minimize congestion, reduce delays, and decrease the likelihood of secondary crashes. CMS are commonly used to communicate information about upstream congestion, estimated travel times, lane closures, disabled vehicles, public safety alerts, and emergency conditions.

The FHWA's approach to CMS message development is based on research-backed practices and is designed to assist CMS operators in creating effective messages for nonrecurring events. This includes guidance on CMS provisions, message construction principles, and strategies for cultivating driver trust in CMS information. Messages are carefully controlled and must follow strict federal and state guidelines to ensure they are concise, easy to read, and consistent, thereby supporting driver comprehension while minimizing distraction.

CMS encompass several subcategories that address different operational needs. Dynamic Message Signs (DMS) are typically permanent installations integrated into traffic management systems for continuous operation along roadways. Portable Message Signs (PMS) are temporary units that can be rapidly deployed for short-term applications such as work zones, incident response, maintenance activities, special events, or emergencies.
