Adaptive traffic signals are advanced traffic management systems designed to optimize traffic flow by responding dynamically to real-time traffic conditions instead of relying on fixed schedules.

Using data from sensors, cameras, and connected vehicle systems, these signals adjust timing patterns to match actual traffic volumes, helping reduce congestion and delays in areas where traffic patterns can shift because of construction, events, or weather.

By improving signal coordination, adaptive systems can enhance safety at intersections by reducing stop-and-go movements and potential conflicts involving vehicles and pedestrians.

Adaptive signals can also support emergency response by prioritizing signal timing when emergency vehicle presence is detected.

Integrated with broader ITS strategies, adaptive signals support real-time monitoring, data analysis, and coordinated management across multiple intersections.
