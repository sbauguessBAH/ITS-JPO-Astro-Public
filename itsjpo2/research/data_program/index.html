Vision

ITS Data Program envisiones cultivating and fostering leading-edge ITS data research and management, data platforms, and supporting systems that enable burdenless access to, sharing, use, and reuse of ITS data and code to facilitate research, deployment, and operations of ITS for a safe, equitable, effective, sustainable, and resilient multimodal transportation network.

Mission

Facilitate effective generation, acquisition, governance, management, and analysis of ITS data and code to advance the state of multimodal ITS research and enable operational deployments focused on ITS JPO-funded efforts.

About the ITS Data Program

Access to ITS data is critical for public agencies, private companies, academia, and anyone who would like to conduct research and create innovative ITS solutions or services. Consistent and timely access to data increases the return on federal investment in ITS research and demonstration projects. Widespread access and use/reuse of data also helps to inform future investment decisions, which enable rapid deployment of innovative ITS technologies at larger scales and generating maximized benefits as a result.

The ITS Data Program focuses on enabling access to core transportation data across the ITS ecosystem. Access to harmonized and high-quality research data is essential to the integration of ITS with connected and automated vehicles, advanced analytics and decision-support systems, and other emerging ITS areas that are highly data dependent. The ITS Data Program, therefore, seeks to foster the adoption of modern technology and best practices essential to trusted data exchanges and effective use, reuse, and analysis of data. As vehicles and travelers cross jurisdictional boundaries and interact with complex systems of systems, data become a key component of and crucial for the next generation of interoperable transportation systems.

How is the ITS Data Program different than other USDOT Data Programs?

This Program is strictly focused on intelligent transportation systems (ITS) data and code, as directed by the latest ITS JPO Strategic Plan. For more information, please visit this website: https://www.its.dot.gov/stratplan2020/index.htm.
