About Enabling ITS Resources

Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS) rely on many aspects of technology, logistics, and bureaucracy to function successfully. Enabling resources, such as US government policy and legislation, guide ITS efforts and assist in the effective implementation of interoperable ITS technology applications. National ITS policies in addition to Architecture, Standards, and Cybersecurity (ASC) resources, comprise the foundational elements or tools needed to enable agencies to plan, design and manage regional ITS developments.

ITS Architecture, Standards, and Cybersecurity research and activities support the resiliency, interoperability, and successful deployment of intelligent transportation systems through the development of ITS standards, specifications, and protocols; maintenance and evolution of the National ITS Architecture reference; and making resources, information, and tools available to stakeholder communities to support secure and cyber-resilient ITS implementations.

The ASC program conducts research and engages relevant organizations to foster a comprehensive set of standards that support the ITS and connected/automated vehicle architecture reference sets that incorporate modern cybersecurity principles for the wide-scale deployment and integration of ITS technologies.

Vision

Enable secure interoperability within a complex, multimodal, connected transportation network, including both vehicles and infrastructure.

One of the main goals for the creation of the National ITS Reference Architecture (ARC-IT) was to define the key interfaces for standardization. Information flows (and their constituent detailed data flows) serve as the basis for much of the standards work on the ITS Standards Program.

Within the framework of the National ITS Reference Architecture, ITS standards developers can identify standards for development that will meet the user’s needs, ITS planners can integrate regional ITS elements using these ITS standards and achieve their interoperability goals, and ITS deployers can select the ITS standards that reduce risk to their deployment and thus help to manage costs and schedule.

ITS Standards

The U.S. DOT established the ITS Standards Program in 1996 to encourage the widespread use of ITS technologies in our Nation's surface transportation systems. ITS standards exist within technologies deployed under the framework of the National ITS Reference Architecture, also known as "ARC-IT" and define how system components interconnect and interact. Because ITS standards are based on open, non-proprietary technology, their use can facilitate the deployment of interoperable ITS systems and make it easier for state and local ITS deployers to develop and deploy regionally integrated transportation systems.

The ITS Standards Program is teaming with standards development organizations (SDOs) and industry groups to accelerate the development and testing of consensus-based standards, protocols, and specifications while working with state and local highway and transit agencies on standards-based ITS implementation strategies.

ITS Reference Architecture

The Architecture Reference for Cooperative and Intelligent Transportation (ARC-IT) provides a common framework for planning, defining, and integrating intelligent transportation systems. It is a mature reference architecture providing a common basis for planners and engineers with differing concerns to conceive, design and implement systems using a common language as a basis for delivering ITS, but does not mandate any specific implementation. It defines "what must be done," not "how it will be done”. An architecture is functionally oriented and not technology-specific which allows the architecture to remain effective over time.

ITS Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computer systems, networks, and data from digital attacks, unauthorized access, and data breaches. ITS manufacturers, integrators, and operators must systematically assess risks at all levels to mitigate the vulnerabilities, malfunctions, or opportunities for exploitation.

The ITS Cybersecurity program was developed in response to this urgent need to protect ITS from cyber-attacks. It makes resources, information, and tools available to stakeholder communities to support secure and cyber- resilient ITS deployments.

Visit the ITS Cybersecurity Page

ITS ASC Enabling Legislation and Policy

Enabling policy and legislation provide guidelines on the adoption and utilization of architecture, standards, and cybersecurity principles in ITS systems. By following them, SDOs, deployers, and OEMs work together to improve interoperability and reliability throughout our transportation systems nationwide.

Visit the Enabling Legislation and Policy Page

ITS ASC Resources and Tools

The ITS ASC Program offers a wide array of valuable resources and tools to enable the successful deployment of ITS efforts across the United States. The primary focus of these resources and tools is to guide and assist deployers as they develop their projects, provide best practices and lessons learned from previous deployments, and increase efficiency and cost-effectiveness.
